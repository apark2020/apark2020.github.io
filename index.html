Some projects I've worked on

An unmistakable advantage: Private high schools and club sports teams make a difference in recruitment of Columbia athletes

Created a scrolly sankey chart that highlights different paths based on the scroll text. Graphic visualizes the distribution of type of high schools (public/private/other) attended by Columbia varsity athletes. Sankey created with RAWGraphs, scrolly + highlighting created using scrollama + JS.

Demographic shift shakes West Harlem amid a surge of luxury developments

Mapped four different variables found in the American Community Survey 5-Year estimates from 2010, 2015, and 2019. The variables mapped are the % of population with high school degrees, the poverty rate, the median rent, and the % of population spending >35% of their income on rent. The data is broken up by census tract. Map created with Mapbox Studio and Javascript.

West Harlem residents face stark disparities in neighborhood access to healthy food

Mapped retail food stores in New York City Council District 7, and classified them into three categories: fresh produce-carrying supermarkets, convenience stores, and Top 30 national food chains. Map created with Mapbox Studio and Javascript.

Gender Disparity in Collegiate Athletics Funding

A deep dive into the widening gap in funding between men's and women's Athletics programs at Columbia University. Using Python, I analyzed Ivy League Athletics programs' financial records published via EADA reports between 2011-2020 and visualized my findings with R and Adobe Illustrator.

Food Gentrification in Morningside Heights

Built an interactive d3 table with data tracking the rapid chainification of the restaurant landscape in Morningside Heights over the last decade. The table shows which restaurant(s) occupied an address near Columbia since 2008, and color-codes each restaurant by the type of business (national chain, local chain, mom and pop stores).

Charged NYPD Officers' Salaries

Analyzed yearly changes in the salaries of 26 NYPD officers with complaints filed against them. Part of a Columbia Daily Specator article awarded finalist in the Society of Professional Journalists Mark of Excellence In-Depth Reporting (Large) category. Data was sourced from NYPD Complaint Data on NYC Open Data, Citywide Payroll Records (also on NYC Open Data), and CAPStat.
